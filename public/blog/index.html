Data Wrangling and Visualization with Snowshoe Hares/Principal Components Analysis (PCA)

By Nathaniel Burola

Data wrangling and visualization with snowshoe hares (Lepus americanus) Snowshore hares, otherwise known as Lepus americanus, are a keystone prey species that play an ecologically important role in northern boreal forests and experience population fluctuations of 8-11 years. This capture-recapture study of snowshoe hares was conducted at 5 locales in the Tanana valley from study sites Tok in the east to Clear in the west from 1999-2012 with a time duration of 13 years.

Hawaiian Land Use, Land Cover, and Watersheds This project depicts Hawaiian land use and land cover for all of the main islands in Hawaii. Categories are utilized and displayed on an interactive map such as urban areas, rural areas, forest, sand, rock, etc to show different areas that are covered in different ways.
